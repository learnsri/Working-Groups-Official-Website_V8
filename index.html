Vision

A Shared Vision for Collaboration and Capacity Building

LEARN36 marked a major milestone in strengthening Sri Lanka’s academic and research network for the digital future.

Launched at the 36th Annual General Meeting of the Lanka Education And Research Network (LEARN), the LEARN Working Groups Platform represents a strategic shift from isolated efforts to coordinated, outcome-driven national collaboration. The platform is designed to strengthen collaboration, coordination, and capacity-building across Sri Lanka’s higher education and research ecosystem. It brings together universities, researchers, policymakers, industry partners, and international collaborators to address priority national challenges through focused and measurable initiatives. Through national and regional engagement, including alignment with the Asia Pacific Advanced Network (APAN), LEARN promotes responsible technology adoption, digital transformation, and inclusive innovation. Together, the LEARN Working Groups drive structured collaboration, shared expertise, and sustainable national impact.

LEARN-led APAN-aligned National + Regional

Strategic Direction and Regional Alignment

Led by the Lanka Education and Research Network (LEARN) and aligned with the Asia Pacific Advanced Network (APAN), the Working Groups Platform supports coordinated national and regional collaboration to strengthen Sri Lanka’s higher education and research ecosystem.

Through structured governance, shared expertise, and regional engagement, the platform enables institutions to address emerging challenges and deliver sustainable, scalable solutions.

Leader

Message from Chairman

Prof. K. L. Wasantha Kumara

As Chairman of LEARN, I view these Working Groups as vital national platforms that strengthen trust, continuity, and long-term value in Sri Lanka’s research and education ecosystem. The AgroTech and Generative AI Working Groups reflect LEARN’s commitment to responsible innovation, inclusive participation, and regional cooperation. By fostering strong governance, strategic alignment, and sustained engagement with APAN, we ensure that these initiatives endure beyond individual projects, supporting national priorities while contributing meaningfully to the Asia-Pacific research and education community.

Leader

Message from CEO

Prof. Roshan Ragel

I envision our Working Groups as catalysts that connect Sri Lanka’s research and education community to the broader Asia-Pacific ecosystem. Through the AgriTech and Generative AI Working Groups, we aim to empower institutions, nurture innovation, and translate emerging technologies into real societal impact. These platforms will strengthen collaboration, shape forward-looking policies, and amplify Sri Lanka’s contributions to APAN. Together, we are building sustainable, future-ready networks that drive productivity, knowledge sharing, and regional leadership.

Leader

Message from CTO

Dr. Asitha Bandaranayake

As CTO of LEARN, my focus is on translating vision into action for national and societal benefit. These groups serve as practical, collaborative platforms for piloting technologies, building capacity, and delivering measurable outcomes in agriculture, education, research, and productivity. By aligning closely with APAN Working Groups, we ensure that Sri Lanka’s initiatives are technically strong, globally connected, and scalable, turning ideas into deployable solutions that create real value for society.
