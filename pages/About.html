About the LEARN Working Groups Platform

The LEARN Working Groups Platform is a national initiative that strengthens collaboration, coordination, and capacity-building across Sri Lanka’s higher education and research ecosystem—bringing universities, researchers, policymakers, and international partners together to solve priority challenges through focused, outcome-oriented collaboration.

Launched at LEARN36 AGM
Focus Action + Outcomes
Alignment Regional + Global

What it is

Launched at LEARN’s 36th Annual General Meeting, the Working Groups represent a strategic shift toward coordinated problem-solving in response to key challenges such as digital transformation, responsible AI adoption, and sustainability.

They provide a structured pathway to translate shared challenges into collaborative research agendas, pilot initiatives, and policy-relevant outcomes.

Strategic shift

A move from disconnected efforts to coordinated problem-solving—designed to translate shared challenges into pilots, research agendas, and policy-relevant outcomes.

Digital transformation Modernizing systems and research infrastructure
Responsible AI adoption Safe, productive, and ethical AI usage
Sustainability Solutions built for long-term impact

Regional and Global Alignment

The platform is aligned with the Asia Pacific Advanced Network (APAN), enabling Sri Lankan institutions to engage in regional research collaboration, open science, and capacity-building across the Asia–Pacific region.

This ensures national initiatives remain globally connected, interoperable, and future-ready.

Lanka Education and Research Network

LEARN is Sri Lanka's National Research and Education Network (NREN), established in 1989 to connect the country's academic and research institutions. As an Internet Service Provider dedicated to the education and research sector, LEARN offers high-speed backbone networks, provides dedicated technical support tailored to research projects, and fosters collaboration among universities and R&D institutions.

Vision

To be a world-class Research and Education Network (REN) in the region, catering to the connection, communication, and collaboration needs of Sri Lanka's research and education community, accelerating economic and social development.

Mission

  • Equity for all: Ensuring fair access to advanced network infrastructure for all stakeholders.
  • More for less: Offering innovative, affordable services to meet research and education needs, promoting fairness.
  • Reimagining Research and Education: Facilitating global connectivity for continuous advancement in learning and innovation.

Governance

The Board of Directors (BoD) of LEARN comprises seventeen members representing each full-member institution. The BoD is chaired by the University Grants Commission representative, with directors appointed annually by their respective institutions.

APAN

Asia–Pacific

Asia Pacific Advanced Network

APAN connects the research and education networks of its member countries/economies across Asia and Oceania, and links them to other research networks worldwide. It coordinates activities related to network technologies, services, and applications among members and with peer international organisations.

What APAN Enables

APAN promotes and facilitates network-enabled research and education activities including research collaboration, knowledge discovery and sharing, tele-health, and natural disaster mitigation.

Role & Structure

APAN refers to both the organisation representing its members and the backbone network connecting member research and education networks. APAN Ltd is the not-for-profit association created to undertake activities on behalf of APAN members.

Core Objectives

  • Coordinate and promote advances in network technologies and network-based applications and services.
  • Support an advanced networking environment for research and education communities across the Asia-Pacific region.
  • Encourage regional and global cooperation to strengthen collaborative innovation.
  • Hold meetings, workshops, and conferences, and support training and capacity-building.
