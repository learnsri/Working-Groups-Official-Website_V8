🤖 Generative AI for Productivity Working Group (GenAI WG)

Advancing Ethical and Practical AI for Education, Research, and Institutional Excellence

The Generative AI for Productivity Working Group (GenAI WG) was established at LEARN36 as a national platform to promote the ethical, practical, and collaborative use of Artificial Intelligence across Sri Lanka’s higher education and research sectors.

Operating under the Lanka Education and Research Network (LEARN), the working group connects universities, technology experts, government agencies, and policy partners to translate global advances in Generative AI into locally relevant, high-impact solutions.

Aligned with the Asia Pacific Advanced Network (APAN) framework, GenAI WG supports regional collaboration, knowledge exchange, and capacity development.

🌍 Why It Matters

Generative AI is transforming how knowledge is created, shared, and applied.

To benefit from this transformation, Sri Lanka requires strong institutional capacity, ethical governance, and inclusive digital tools.

GenAI WG supports institutions to achieve:

✅ Responsible AI adoption
✅ Improved teaching and research productivity
✅ Strong academic integrity
✅ Inclusive digital access
✅ Transparent governance

Through coordinated national efforts, the working group strengthens Sri Lanka’s readiness for the AI-driven future.

Through APAN engagement, GenAI WG also positions Sri Lanka as an emerging regional hub for responsible AI practices in higher education.

Our Role

GenAI WG provides a structured national forum to turn discussion into practical outcomes—guidance, pilots, and adoption support—so institutions can apply Generative AI safely, responsibly, and with measurable value.
