🌱 Agriculture Technology Working Group (AgriTech WG)

Driving Digital Innovation for Sustainable Agriculture in Sri Lanka

The Agriculture Technology Working Group (AgriTech WG) was established at LEARN36 as Sri Lanka’s first national platform dedicated to advancing digital and technology-enabled agriculture.

Operating under the Lanka Education and Research Network (LEARN), the working group brings together universities, researchers, and industry practitioners to apply data science, IoT, and AI in strengthening agricultural productivity, sustainability, and research collaboration.

Aligned with the Asia Pacific Advanced Network (APAN) framework, AgriTech WG connects Sri Lanka’s agricultural research community with regional and international partners.

🤝
Through this platform, members exchange expertise and jointly develop solutions for real-world agricultural challenges.

Our Role

AgriTech WG functions as a national forum for collaboration and innovation, providing a structured platform.

Mission

To support interdisciplinary research and collaboration in agriculture through the application of digital technologies.

Interdisciplinary Digital agriculture

Objectives

  • Support research and knowledge sharing in areas such as precision agriculture, data analytics, and agri-supply-chain management
  • Facilitate collaboration among Sri Lankan institutions and regional partners through APAN
  • Develop and publish a working group charter, terms of reference, and a project roadmap
  • Initiate pilot projects and conduct regular meetings and knowledge-sharing sessions
  • Establish mechanisms for ongoing collaboration and data exchange among members

🌍 Why AgriTech WG Matters

Sri Lanka’s agriculture sector faces major challenges, including climate variability, limited resources, post-harvest losses, and fragmented data systems.

Digital agriculture transforms these constraints into actionable insights and scalable solutions—supporting:

✅ Better decision-making
✅ Reduced waste
✅ Climate resilience
✅ Stronger research collaboration
✅ Sustainable farming practices

AgriTech WG was formed following a national workshop involving academics, researchers, and industry experts to identify priorities and research gaps and to define a coordinated national approach.

🤝
Through this platform, members exchange expertise and jointly develop solutions for real-world agricultural challenges.

Looking Ahead

A long-term national commitment—built to produce scalable and sustainable outcomes.

The Agriculture Technology Working Group represents a long-term national commitment to transforming Sri Lanka’s agriculture through research, innovation, and collaboration. By uniting expertise across disciplines and borders, AgriTech WG aims to deliver practical, scalable, and sustainable solutions that benefit farmers, researchers, and the broader economy.
